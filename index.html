I picked up most of my development skills through online courses and coaching from friends further along the path, and sharpened the fundamentals studying Computer Science at the University of Rivers State.

Somewhere along the way, I noticed something: young people around me were sitting on a goldmine of opportunity in AI, and didn't know it yet. That's what pulled me into teaching — not just to build things myself, but to hand the tools to others so they could build too.

"If I build something, it could benefit some people. But if I teach people how to build, a lot more people will benefit — and the world will be better."

I'll know I'm winning when I've shipped an app or website that competes on a global scale, or empowered 1,000 young people with what I know. Ideally, both.
